# Docs Studio — positioning and use cases

## Core promise

> Your AI can write. Docs Studio makes it follow the brief.

Docs Studio is an AI document workspace for reports, proposals, specifications, and technical deliverables that must follow a brief, rubric, template, or set of rules.

It is not a general-purpose word processor. Word and Google Docs remain strong choices for open-ended editing, collaboration, and final polishing. Docs Studio is focused on the step where requirements become a structured document.

## Four practical markets

### Universities and training programs

Turn a course guide or rubric into a document students can actually complete.

Examples:

- workshop and lab reports;
- capstone submissions;
- rubric-led research papers;
- technical training assignments.

Rules may include required sections, word limits, equations, tables, citation instructions, or evidence requirements.

### Consultancies and agencies

Make every client deliverable follow the brief and the house style.

Examples:

- RFP and proposal responses;
- client strategy documents;
- research summaries;
- monthly or quarterly reports.

Rules may include brand voice, scope, deliverables, exclusions, reusable sections, and approved terminology.

### Engineering, operations, and compliance

Turn requirements into documents that do not forget important parts.

Examples:

- technical specifications;
- standard operating procedures;
- change requests;
- incident and audit reports.

Rules may include mandatory fields, evidence, risk sections, approval steps, and controlled vocabulary.

### Repeatable document batches

Create one approved document many times with different values. This is a strong product extension for reports by student, client, site, project, or installation.

The future batch workflow should support:

1. a locked document template;
2. mapped variables such as `{{name}}`, `{{site}}`, or `{{date}}`;
3. per-document validation against the same rules;
4. batch export to DOCX/PDF;
5. a failure report instead of silently producing incomplete copies.

This is a product opportunity, not a claim that the complete batch engine exists today.

## Human and agent surfaces

### For humans

- load the brief or reference files;
- shape the document on a paper-like canvas;
- inspect proposed changes;
- accept or reject edits;
- export the final document.

### For agents

MCP exposes the document surface so an external AI can create, read, draft, revise, and export documents using the same workspace model. The agent integration should complement the editor rather than bypass human review.

## Competitive framing

Do not say Docs Studio is a better general editor than Word or Google Docs. Say it is more focused for documents with explicit requirements.

| General editor | Docs Studio |
|---|---|
| Open-ended writing and collaboration | Brief-led document generation |
| Manual or broad AI assistance | Rules, templates, and structured context |
| Final editing surface | Draft, review, and requirement-aware workflow |
| Many possible document types | Focused reports, proposals, specifications, and technical documents |

## Language to use

Use: brief, rubric, template, requirements, working draft, proposed edit, structured document, review, export.

Avoid: Word replacement, Google Docs competitor, guaranteed grades, perfect APA, autonomous writer, best-in-class, revolutionary, or unsupported time savings.
